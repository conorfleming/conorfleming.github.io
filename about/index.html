About

As an engineering scientist at Coanda, I focus on computational modelling of complex fluid flows to solve immediate industrial challenges. Over the last ten years I have gained a broad range of experience as a mechanical engineer in academic and industrial settings.

My doctoral research at the University of Oxford examined the performance of tidal stream turbines in highly-turbulent open channel flows via CFD modelling.

As a tunnel ventilation engineer with Atkins and subsequently SNC-Lavalin, I developed expertise in numerical and physical modelling of heat and mass transport through tunnels and buildings. I have experience at all stages of major infrastructure projects, from bid and concept design, through detailed design development and coordination, to construction support, system commissioning and project close-out. In 2019 I co-authored design guidelines on road tunnel ventilation systems as a member of a World Road Association working group.

My areas of interest include ventilation of tunnels and other complex environments, treatment of mine tailings, and wind and tidal turbine performance.

Qualifications & Associations

  • BE in Mechanical Engineering, National University of Ireland, Galway
  • DPhil in Engineering Science, University of Oxford, UK
  • Professional Engineer (P.Eng.) in British Columbia and Ontario
  • Chartered Engineer with the Institution of Mechanical Engineers (CEng MIMechE)
